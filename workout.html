CHEST

SUMMARY

Best exercises:

  • Incline dumbbell press is the best overall builder
  • Cable flys provide constant tension and great stretch to the pecs
  • Chest press machines as machines provide stability throughout the range of motion and make it easy to push to failure

WHAT TO AVOID

  • Flat Bench is not the most ideal for chest growth but for stregnth
  • Although Dumbell Flat press might not be up there it has been
    recently proven to have some benefits in chest growth

BACK

SUMMARY

    Train the whole back, not just lats

  • Vertical pulls (lat pulldown)
  • Horizontal rows (mid-back)

    • Structure of the workout:

    1. Lat pulldown: lat width
    2. Row variation: mid-back thickness
    3. Another row/pulldown: extra volume
    4. Rear delt exercise: shoulder balance
    5. Shrugs: traps
    6. Biceps curls (2 types)

    Use different grips

  • Targets different parts of the back

SHOULDERS

SUMMARY

    Shoulders have 3 parts:

  • Front delts
  • Lateral (side) delts
  • Rear delts
  • Lateral delts are most important for aesthetics

    • Best exercises:

  • Lateral raises (top priority)
  • Cable lateral raises (constant tension)
  • Rear delt flys (balance and posture)
  • Overhead press (overall mass)

    • Mid-tier exercises:

  • Dumbbell shoulder press
  • Machine press (more stable but less activation)

    • Least effective:

  • Exercises that mainly target front delts (already overworked)
  • Poor form lateral raises (using momentum)

BICEPS

SUMMARY

    Best exercises:

  • Incline dumbbell curls → biggest stretch (top tier)
  • Preacher curls → strong contraction
  • Cable curls → constant tension

    • Good but not elite:

  • Barbell curls
  • Hammer curls (more brachialis/forearms emphasis)

    • Less effective:

  • Cheating curls (too much momentum)
  • Exercises with poor stretch or poor tension

TRICEPS

SUMMARY

    Best exercise:

  • Overhead cable tricep extension
  • Effectively targets all 3 heads, especially the long head

    • Other top-tier exercises:

  • Skull crushers
  • Cable pushdowns
  • Dips / close-grip bench (for heavier loading)

    • Least effective:

  • Dumbbell kickbacks (poor tension, hard to overload)

    • Key principle:

  • Choose exercises with good stretch
  • Maintain constant tension
  • Allow easy progression over time

LEGS

SUMMARY

    Use 2 types of movements:

  • Compound (heavy): squats, RDL/rear deadlift
  • Isolation movements: leg extensions, curls

    • Structure:

  • Squat (main lift)
  • Hinge (RDL)
  • Leg press or lunge
  • Leg extension
  • Hamstring curl
  • Calves

    • Full range of motion matters

  • Deep squats = more growth

ABDOMINALS

SUMMARY

    Abs = mostly low body fat

  • You won’t see abs unless you’re lean enough

    • Diet is the biggest factor

  • Calorie control and high protein = fat loss + muscle retention

    • Train abs like any muscle

  • Use resistance like weighted crunches, leg raises
  • Avoid only doing high reps with no load

    • Add cardio if needed

  • Helps burn extra calories for fat loss

    • Common mistakes:

  • Only doing ab exercises
  • Not tracking calories
  • Expecting spot reduction
